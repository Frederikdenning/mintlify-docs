**Life Cycle Assessment (LCA)** thinking helps teams brainstorm ideas based on actual environmental impact rather than assumptions.

| Lifecycle Stage | Questions to Spark Ideas |
|------------------|--------------------------|
| üåæ **Materials** | Can we replace virgin inputs with renewable or recycled ones? |

| ‚ôªÔ∏è **End of Life** | What happens after use ‚Äî repair, reuse, recycling? |

## Methods to Generate Sustainable Product Ideas

### 1. **Problem Reversal**
Instead of asking ‚Äúhow can we make this product greener?‚Äù, start with  
> ‚ÄúWhat makes this product unsustainable ‚Äî and how do we eliminate that?‚Äù

For example, single-use packaging, toxic coatings, or non-repairable parts all signal opportunity for innovation.

### 2. **Life Cycle Mapping**
Draw your product‚Äôs hypothetical journey ‚Äî from raw material to disposal ‚Äî and mark high-impact areas (energy, emissions, waste).  
Then brainstorm alternatives for each stage.  

> This simple visual process often reveals quick wins, like switching to lighter materials or modular design.

### 3. **Analogy Thinking**

- Fashion‚Äôs shift to **circular textiles** inspires electronics modularity.  
- Food packaging‚Äôs **biobased materials** can inspire sustainable hardware casings.
