## 2. What LCAs measure that carbon calculators miss

Basic carbon calculators only look at direct transport emissions. LCAs expand this scope.\
They consider the **entire lifecycle of logistics activities**, including:

- Vehicle and fuel production
- Infrastructure (warehouses, terminals)
- Maintenance and end-of-life disposal
- Packaging materials and return flows
- Combined multi-leg shipments (road, rail, sea, air)

## 4. Practical applications in logistics operations

### Smarter mode selection

LCAs make it possible to calculate impact per ton-kilometer across modes.\
Teams can see the verified difference between using long-haul rail instead of truck, or marine biofuel instead of conventional bunker fuel.

### Packaging and return logistics

By quantifying packaging‚Äôs lifecycle impact, LCAs can support shifts to reusable or modular systems ‚Äî reducing waste while improving cost control.

### Supplier and carrier evaluation

When third-party carriers provide verified LCA data, procurement teams gain a more accurate view of supplier performance and compliance.

### Warehouse energy optimization

LCAs include the energy and material footprint of storage and handling facilities, making operational energy use part of the same dataset as transport impacts.

## 5. Why this matters now

Logistics is rapidly becoming a regulatory and reputational focal point.\
Governments and clients are requiring transparency under frameworks such as:

- **EU CSRD** (Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive)
- **ISO 14083** for greenhouse gas quantification in transport chains
- **GHG Protocol Scope 3** for value chain emissions reporting

LCAs provide the verified, traceable data these frameworks demand ‚Äî and help logistics departments get ahead of compliance rather than react to it later.

> Transparency isn‚Äôt a marketing feature anymore; it‚Äôs a license to operate.
