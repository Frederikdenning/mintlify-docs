As sustainability becomes a core business variable, finance teams are expected to incorporate environmental factors into models, forecasts, and investment planning.  
Structured lifecycle insights make this possible. By pairing financial data with a clear view of material use, resource intensity, and supply chain impact, finance departments gain a broader and more accurate basis for decision-making.

## The changing scope of the finance function
Finance teams today manage far more than traditional cost and revenue planning. Regulatory pressure, investor expectations, and carbon-related risks have expanded the scope of analysis.  
Key questions now include:

- Which products or processes carry the highest environmental exposure?  
- How do upcoming regulations influence pricing, margins, or future tax liabilities?  
- Which investments strengthen the organisation’s long-term resilience?  

Structured environmental data helps answer these questions with the same rigor applied to financial metrics.

---

## How sustainability data supports financial strategy

### 1. Identifying non-financial risk
Many environmental risks translate into future financial exposure. Quantifying material footprints, carbon intensity, or waste flows allows finance teams to assign clear cost implications, anticipate regulatory changes, and adjust planning accordingly.

### 2. Strengthening procurement decisions
Finance teams involved in purchasing oversight can use sustainability metrics to compare suppliers beyond unit cost. Lifecycle insights reveal the total cost of ownership, including resource intensity and potential environmental liabilities.

### 3. Evaluating capital investments
Investments in new technology, infrastructure, or product development benefit from an understanding of long-term environmental performance. This helps determine which initiatives reduce operating costs, improve efficiency, or enhance eligibility for sustainable finance instruments.

### 4. Supporting ESG and CSRD reporting
Upcoming reporting requirements demand verifiable, consistent data. When sustainability metrics are centralised, finance can produce clear, audit-ready disclosures without relying on estimates or manual consolidation.

### 5. Integrating environmental factors into financial planning
Project profitability increasingly depends on exposure to carbon pricing, material volatility, and regulatory shifts. By incorporating environmental indicators into forecasting, finance teams can evaluate margins under multiple scenarios and support more resilient planning.
