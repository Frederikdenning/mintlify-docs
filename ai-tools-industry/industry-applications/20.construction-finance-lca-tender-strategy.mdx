## Why tender scoring now depends on environmental data  
Public procurement and corporate clients increasingly require verified data on:

- Embodied carbon  
- Material sourcing and waste  
- Compliance with EN 15804  
- Carbon-reduction plans across the project lifecycle  

Environmental sections often represent **up to 30% of tender scoring**, making LCA-backed data as important as the financial proposal itself.

## How LCA strengthens tender competitiveness

### 1. **Competitive pricing with carbon intelligence**  
Finance can model a ‚Äúcarbon-aware‚Äù bid by linking LCA outputs to:

- Internal carbon pricing  
- Supplier impact variations  
- Future tax exposure  
- Material substitution scenarios  

This avoids underpricing risk while keeping bids competitive under sustainability scoring rules.

---

### 2. **Instant credibility with verifiers and evaluators**  
Submitting quantified impact data (LCA results, EPDs, or EN 15804-aligned figures) signals:

- Transparency  
- Technical maturity  
- Reduced verification workload for evaluators  

This often leads to higher environmental scoring and fewer clarifications during the review process.

---

### 3. **Margin protection through early insight**  
LCAs uncover hidden cost drivers:

- High-impact materials that also carry high financial volatility  
- Inefficient processes that create waste  
- Suppliers lacking verified documentation  

Finance avoids pursuing bids that look good on paper but erode margin once sustainability requirements kick in.

## Simple indicators finance teams can track

- **% of bids including verified impact data**  
- **Carbon-adjusted bid variance vs. baseline**  
- **Tender win rate when LCA is included**  
- **Margin stability on sustainability-scored projects**

These metrics help finance evaluate how sustainability integration affects bottom-line performance.
