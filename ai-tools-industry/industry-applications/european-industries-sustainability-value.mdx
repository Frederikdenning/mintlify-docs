## Why Sustainability Insight Matters More in Europe

Europe’s policy landscape is structured, ambitious, and fast-moving.  
Companies are now expected to show **traceable, comparable environmental data** across products, supply chains, and operations.

Key frameworks reshaping the region include:

- **Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive (CSRD)**  
- **EU Taxonomy**  
- **Product Environmental Footprint (PEF)**  
- **Ecodesign for Sustainable Products Regulation (ESPR)**  
- **Green Public Procurement requirements**

<Callout icon="scale" color="#22C55E">
In the EU, sustainability reporting is shifting from narrative to **evidence** — and lifecycle data is at the center of that shift.
</Callout>

## European Industries Seeing the Biggest Benefits

### Construction & Building Materials  
A sector under intense scrutiny for embodied carbon and material transparency.  
Sustainability insights support data for design choices, tenders, and verified documentation such as EPDs.

### Manufacturing & Industrial Production  
Manufacturers face growing pressure to measure supply chain emissions and improve resource efficiency.  
Clear lifecycle data helps teams compare production routes, optimize materials, and prepare for border-adjustment policies.

### Food & Agriculture  
Food systems are directly linked to climate and land-use goals.  
Lifecycle data enables carbon labeling, farm-to-shelf transparency, and more sustainable sourcing decisions.

### Packaging & Consumer Goods  
With circularity rules tightening, teams use sustainability tools to compare packaging options, reduce waste, and document improvements clearly.

### Textiles & Fashion  
The EU Strategy for Sustainable Textiles introduces new expectations for durability, recyclability, and product-level transparency — all of which rely on measurable lifecycle insights.

### Electronics & Technology  
Digital product passports and critical raw material rules make lifecycle information essential for responsible product design and supply chain visibility.

<Callout icon="globe" color="#16A34A">
Across Europe, industries that rely on complex materials or deep supply chains gain the most from clear, comparable environmental insight.
</Callout>

## How Regulation Strengthens the Business Case

Europe’s regulatory ecosystem provides both pressure and opportunity.  
When teams understand lifecycle impacts early, they can:

- prepare for digital product passports  
- align with CSRD and corporate ESG reporting  
- improve eligibility for green procurement  
- differentiate products with measurable performance  
- build verifiable sustainability claims for marketing and sales  

These benefits compound across entire portfolios — not just flagship products.
